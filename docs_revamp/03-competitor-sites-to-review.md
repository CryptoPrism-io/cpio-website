# Competitor / Reference Sites — from the Market Primer

Source: primer Section 08 ("Go Deeper"). Reviewed via rendered screenshots (Playwright, 1440px) where accessible; `defillama.com` and `trendlyne.com` blocked automated access (Cloudflare/CloudFront bot-check) — notes for those two rely on the primer's own description.

## glassnode.com — closest global analogue
Clean, light, corporate-institutional design (white/grey, black CTA). Headline: **"Pioneering digital asset market intelligence."** Sub-copy: "Built for investing, trading, and research." Immediately below the fold: **"Trusted by industry leaders"** logo bar — Coinbase Institutional, Binance, CME Group, Grayscale, ARK Invest. No product screenshot in the hero at all — the trust bar *is* the pitch.
**Takeaway:** institutional credibility can be communicated through restraint (whitespace, logos, no jargon) rather than density. CryptoPrism has zero trust-logo equivalent anywhere on site.

## nansen.ai — "narrow product, big outcome"
Dark, neon-green-on-black, heavy product screenshot in hero (live token screener table). Headline: **"Trade Everything Onchain With AI."** Backed-by logos (a16z, GIC, Sequoia-style) directly under the fold. Very close to CryptoPrism's own dark/green aesthetic already — validates the visual direction, but Nansen leads with "trade" (execution) not "research," interesting contrast to Messari.
**Takeaway:** CryptoPrism's dark-green terminal aesthetic is on-trend for this category, not a liability. The gap is copy/positioning, not visual style.

## tokenterminal.com — the B2B DaaS proof point
Editorial/magazine feel — huge serif-adjacent display type ("Blockchain data you can understand"), warm photography (architecture, trees) mixed with a live financial-statement data card. Very different visual register from the dark-terminal aesthetic of Nansen/CryptoPrism — reads more "publication" than "trading tool."
**Takeaway:** the B2B/data-licensing pitch doesn't have to look like a trading terminal — it can look like a research publication. Relevant if CryptoPrism builds a distinct page/mode for the institutional/DaaS audience.

## messari.io — the positioning template
**Headline: "Where institutions go to understand crypto."** This is close to verbatim what CryptoPrism's deck claims to be but the site doesn't say. Sub-copy: "Research, evaluate, and monitor crypto assets with analyst-verified intelligence across 40K+ assets. Start exploring for free, upgrade when you need more." Dark, blue-accented, live terminal screenshot in hero, freemium CTA ("Start Exploring" + "Talk to Sales").
**Takeaway:** this is the single closest template for what CryptoPrism's homepage headline/positioning should probably look like — institutional framing, freemium-to-enterprise CTA pairing (not "still free for now"), analyst/verified-data credibility language.

## altfins.com — the achievable-scale direct comparable
Busy, dense, utilitarian — teal/dark-green, tag-cloud of features above the fold ("Crypto Trading Signals," "AI Chart Patterns," "Coin Picks," "Copilot"), video walkthrough link, then a "For Traders / For Partners" split immediately below. Much more feature-list-driven and less polished than Messari/Nansen/Glassnode.
**Takeaway:** proof that a research tool can succeed on functional density rather than premium design polish — but also a visual cautionary tale: this is the least differentiated-looking of the set, reads generic-SaaS. CryptoPrism should aim above this bar, not toward it.

## liquide.life — Indian retail research app
Dark, bold sans headline **"Empowering India's Wealth Journey,"** SEBI registration number displayed prominently near the top (`SEBI RA INH000009816`) — direct trust/compliance signal above the fold. App-store badges immediately under the headline. Stacked card carousel showing sub-products (Liquide One, Dynamic Growth, India Growth).
**Takeaway:** displaying the regulatory registration number itself, right at the top, is a trust device CryptoPrism could consider once relevant (compliance-grade positioning is already a deck claim — "DPDP-ready architecture" — but isn't surfaced as a credential anywhere visible).

## Cross-cutting takeaways for the revamp
1. **Messari's headline formula is the one to beat/adapt**: "Where institutions go to understand crypto" + freemium CTA pairing. CryptoPrism's current "Think Like You. Trade Like a Machine" reads retail-execution, not institutional-research.
2. **Trust logos / registration credentials are a recurring device** (Glassnode's institutional-logo bar, Liquide's SEBI number) that CryptoPrism's site currently has none of, despite the deck having credible claims to lean on (founder pedigree, DPDP-readiness, out-of-sample evidence).
3. **Dark/green terminal aesthetic is validated** by Nansen — CryptoPrism doesn't need a visual overhaul, it needs copy/positioning/trust-signal work layered onto the direction it already has.
4. **altFINS is the "don't end up here" bar** — functional but generic-looking; useful as a floor, not a ceiling.
